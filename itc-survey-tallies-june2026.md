# ITC Community Tech Survey — full tallies (recalculated June 2026)

Source: raw Google Form responses CSV ("Community Perspective on Technology in Our Schools"), 78 responses, April 6 – May 6, 2026.

## Inclusion rule (decided by Emily, June 2026)
Counted: respondents who name at least one Concord public school (Willard, Thoreau, Alcott, CMS, CCHS).
**n = 71.**
Excluded (7): 2 private-school-only families, 4 Carlisle-only families, 1 retired CPS teacher.
Note: two "Parent, Teacher" respondents at Concord schools were **kept** (they are current CPS parents). Flag if they should also be excluded. One family listed "CCHS, Carlisle Public but will attend CCHS in two years" — kept because they name CCHS.

## Homepage headline stats (n = 71)
- **90%** flagged cell phones or screen time during the school day as a concern (64/71 selected at least one of the two)
- **77%** answered "No" to having a clear understanding of personal-device-policy enforcement (55/71; Yes = 16/71 = 23%)
- **82%** said a K-8 Technology Advisory Committee would be "Extremely valuable" (58/71)

## Concerns (multi-select, % of 71)
| Concern (full survey wording) | n | % |
|---|---|---|
| Excessive screen time during school hours | 53 | 75% |
| Student cell phone access or use during the school day | 51 | 72% |
| Online safety and exposure to inappropriate content | 50 | 70% |
| Use of screen time in schools during free time or as a reward | 46 | 65% |
| Amount of time spent doing homework on school-issued computers | 38 | 54% |
| Implementation of AI tools for student use (e.g., generative AI for assignments or tutoring support) | 33 | 46% |
| Data privacy and protection of student information | 32 | 45% |
| Reliance on personal smartphones for school activities (e.g., QR codes for signups or contests) | 31 | 44% |
| Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools by educators (e.g., for grading, lesson planning, or student evaluation) | 28 | 39% |
| Use of social media by school clubs, teams, or programs for required communications | 26 | 37% |
| Limited digital literacy or responsible technology use in education | 19 | 27% |
| I am not concerned about school-based technology use | 1 | 1% |

Write-in concerns (verbatim themes, not auto-publish): video games on school computers during school/homework; lack of opportunity to hold a book or use a pencil; Smartpass use at CMS; technology use in the Willard elementary library class.

The homepage shows the top 8 of these (excluding "reliance on smartphones," "social media by clubs," and "AI by educators" bars). Worth revisiting which 8 appear, since AI-by-educators (39%) now outranks social-media-by-clubs (37%).
